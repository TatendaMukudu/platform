## What the alignment doc establishes (one-glance)
- **Alignment = coherence between behavior over time and stated aims** — directional, not a score, never obedience.
- **Three frames play different roles:** member goals = engine, team goals = context, org values = guardrails. Not a hierarchy.
- **Directional state vocabulary** (Converging / Sustaining / Stalled / Diverging / Unanchored / Unknown) instead of any 0–100 number.
- **Conflict doctrine** that can side *against* the team or org, and treats a dignified exit as a valid outcome.
- **Briefing is the product**, not scores. Every briefing answers: what we're seeing / why / how it aligns (member·team·org) / what to try next.
- **Design law:** "If a screen lets you sort people by an alignment number, the screen is wrong."
